Penn State University - Geospatial Intelligence Analytics Certificate

May 2020 - May 2021

Intelligence Analysis and Cultural Geography - GEOG 571

The purpose of this course was to explore the most prominent threats to the civil security of the United States from an intelligence perspective. The aim was to teach the importance of geography to the intelligence process through the use both quantitative and qualitative spatial analysis. Moreover, the course was designed to teach the importance of human geography to these civil security issues. These issues included security at the US/Mexican border, international terrorism, and organized crime.

Remote Sensing Image Analysis and Application - GEOG 883

The primary focus of this course was on remotely sensed data for geospatial applications. In this course, I developed my analytical workflow skills with a variety of software packages including, ArcGIS 10.8, ArcGIS Pro 2.7, ArcGIS Online, eCognition, and Google Earth Engine. These workflows included data acquisition from openly sourced repositories, image enhancement, automated extraction of remotely sensed data, and accuracy assessment. Additionally, this course developed my critical thinking skills through exploration of real-world problems and through consideration of the limitations within the remote sensing instruments being used to solve those problems.

GIS for the Geospatial Professional - GEOG 884

The primary focus of this course was to further develop GIS skills in the context of a geospatial intelligence professional. In this course, I continued using ArcGIS 10.8, ArcGIS Pro 2.7, and ArcGIS Online, but I also gained experience using other software packages such as GeoDa, CARTO, and QGIS. Lessons covered in this course included how GIS and GEOINT integrate, vector analysis using proximity and overlay operations, raster terrain analysis, data quality/standards, and recent developments in GIS such as space-time integration and web-GIS.

Advanced Analytic Methods for Geospatial Intelligence - GEOG 885

The primary focus of this course was to challenge students to think within the geospatial intelligence domain. Using the Structured Geospatial Analytical Method (SGAM), students were able to walk through two challenging case studies, the 2002 DC Sniper Case and the 2003 death of Assistant US Attorney Johnathan Luna. Through the duration of the course, I walked through the different SGAM steps within both case studies using a variety of techniques. These techniques included brainstorming, key assumption checks, quality information checks, and Analysis of Competing Hypotheses (ACH). Output of these various steps/techniques were data discovery/validation/management, visualizations, and final maps and reports.

Culminating Experiences in Geospatial Intelligence - GEOG 594A & GEOG 594B

The primary focus of this two-part capstone course was to synthesize the knowledge of the previous courses into one final student project. I was required to develop an analytic question pertaining to a real-world GEOINT problem. From there, deliverables included a grounding and exploration literature search, hypotheses/method assumptions & limitation presentation, evidence and analysis to my selected real-world problem, and finally a conclusion in the form of a verbal presentation and a paper. I am presently in GEOG 594B and my analytic question is over nuclear power plants in Texas.

In this course, I developed knowledge over GIS and it components, scale/map projections, data sources, and common GIS operations. Lab work taught me the fundamentals of Esri's ArcMap and ArcCatalog.

GNSS in the Geosciences - GEOG 352

The primary focus of this course was to serve as an introduction to Geodesy and GNSS in the geosciences. Lessons in this course included the fundamentals of GPS/satellites, traditional/modern survey methods, and lab work using survey equipment and Trimble handhelds.

Remote Sensing of the Environment - ESSM 444

The primary focus of this course was to serve as an introduction to remote sensing characteristics and methods. In this course, I developed knowledge over different remote sensing platforms such as aerial & satellite imagery and their respective sensors. I learned about the properties of the electromagnetic spectrum in relation to remote sensing. Additionally, I gained introductory knowledge of LiDAR and SONAR remote sensing sensors. Lab work used ArcMap and ENVI software packages.

Spatial Databases and Programming - ESSM 459

The primary focus of this course was to learn the fundamentals of programming in the context of integration with GIS. Lessons began with learning VBA within excel culminating to writing Python code for ArcMap. Lessons included the basics of arcpy and writing geoprocessing functions within python.

Advanced GIS for Resource Management - ESSM 462

The primary focus of this course was to build on the GIS skills developed in ESSM 351. Lessons included distance/density calculations, geodatabase management interpolation, linear-referencing, map algebra, and the spatial analyst extension within ArcMap.
